Elements of Physical Fantasy Power Rankings

Because trash talk is better with stats to back it up.

Week 7

After a week of hiatus, power rankings are back again. I could come up with an excuse, but I actually just forgot to run them last week. The current standings show an unusual level of parity, quite unfamiliar from years past. Half of the regular season is over, and yet everyone has a good shot at making the playoffs.

The Suspenders (Ryne) vs. The Big Breesy (Zach)

90.6 - 116.8

After a slow start, Zach is now on a two game winning streak. Yes Zach started Andre Ellington who scored exactly 0 points, and yes Zach sat Mark Ingram who scored 28.5 points, but it didn't matter in the end. Thanks to the usual Gronk-smash and volume-based Antonio Brown smack-down, The Suspenders had almost no chance, lacking their RB1, RB2, and yes, their RB3. Some solid wavier-wire moves weren't enough to cover the loss in starting talent. With the courts (temporarily) back on Zeke's side, The Suspenders look to rebound next week with at least one starting RB.

For Whom The Bell Tolls (Rex) vs. The Broken Knee Squad (Frank)

121.3 - 97.8

Another team who faced a rocky start, FWTBT has notched a second consecutive win, and this time versus the league leader. An all-around solid game, anchored by LeVeon Bell's 27.6 point, 32-touch performance, casts a favorable light on the future of this scrappy team. 70+ yard touchdown runs by Fournette are apparently not enough to offset single-digit outings by T.Y. Hilton, Hogan, and Walker. The Broken Knee Squad was still able to put up a respectable score, and remains the team-to-beat going forward.

Brees Company (Brad) vs. Scrubby Squad (Russell)

78.2 - 137.9

The lone bright spot in this massacre for Brees Company is the emergence of Evan Engram from the wasteland that is the Giants receiving corps, and the resurrection of Cousins. The rest of the team failed to reach double digits. Granted it was a hard bye-week, but Michael Thomas and Devante Parker combined for a whopping 2.6 points. Brees will look to snap their two game losing streak with a roster closer to full health this week. Rookie sensation Deshaun Watson produced another outstanding outing, and so did literally everyone else on the Scrubby Squad, except Gillislee. With AP lighting up the bench, the Scrubs will have some hard start/sit decisions going forward. Tied for the lead in their division, the Scrubs are looking to break away from the pack.

Miami Hurricanes (Felix) vs. Jet Quenchers (Laura)

100.6 - 104.6

One of two great nail-biters, this matchup got a little too close for comfort for the Quenchers. A poor performance by Matt Ryan was offset by the apparently-not-hurt-but-benched-last-week Carlos Hyde. Strong scores all around, except for Amari "I often score 1 point" Cooper, helped secure the W. Meanwhile, a 20 point defensive effort by the Ravens almost put the Hurricanes on top this week. Adequate performances by Ajayi and Mike "my weekly score is a sine wave" Evans weren't quite enough to bump the Hurricanes up to .500.

The Brady Bunch (Meghan) vs. Krispy Kareem (Matt)

96.9 - 96.7

Wow what a roller coaster this game was. The Brady Bunch, holding two kickers and Seattle D through their bye, was able to eke out a 0.2 point victory. With no stand-out players this week, the D/ST-less Brady Bunch was able to survive on mediocrity. On the losing side of this matchup, Krispy Kareem suffered a devastating loss to their starting QB, A-a-ron Rodgers. Sitting at .500 and without their top-tier QB, the Kareems may not be able to R-E-L-A-X as Hunt has crashed down to Earth in recent weeks. The upside of Nelson is severely dampened by the noodle arm of the Packer's QB Brett Hundley. Instead, the Krisps may need to lean heavily on their RBs.
